Eyes in Disney cartoons will be able to respond to light

To add realism to the appearance of the characters, Disney Research is working on a method that allows the eyes to respond to light. As Pascal Berard, a studio representative, notes, you can use an ordinary eye model for minor characters, but for the main characters you have to do them manually each time, which takes a lot of time and effort. Read more - on video under the cut.



This method will not only reduce the amount of work when creating an image of cartoon characters, but can also be used in ophthalmology.



Last year, Disney Research presented another eye-related technology. This is PAPILLON , a technology for 3D printing of the most expressive animated eyes for interactive characters, robots and toys.
