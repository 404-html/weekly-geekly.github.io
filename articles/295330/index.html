Amazon founder Jeff Bezos is no longer the best leader. Harvard Business Review Rating



Last year, Amazon CEO Jeff Bezos ranked first in the Harvard Business Review executive rankings. In this place, he left the leadership position, leaving immediately to 87th place. First place is now occupied by the head of the pharmaceutical company Novo from Denmark, Lars Sorensen, writes "Kommersant". The second and third places are taken by Cisco Systems CEO John Chambers and Inditex head Pablo Isla.

The rating by the magazine Harvard Business Review is compiled by analyzing a list of 1.2 thousand different companies, with an analysis of the work of 907 different executives. According to the results of the evaluation of the list, a rating is created of the 100 most successful managers, more precisely, of the general directors.
