Networking, workshops and round tables for IT specialists: how to organize?

It has long been in the West, and more recently in our country in Russia, communities of developers and IT specialists began to appear. Often their informal meetings are held in various coworkings and open spaces. They meet about once every two weeks and discuss the latest news and trends in the high-tech world.

We will talk about three types of such meetings: networking, workshops and round tables.



Networking or ‚Äúget-togethers‚Äù are the most informal meetings where participants gather to get to know each other and share the projects they lead. At such meetings, guests are offered refreshments and snacks (sometimes light alcohol with pizza). They are useful for those who want to find people to implement their project, to learn a lot about the IT field, or to ask advice from more experienced participants.

The workshop is a collective learning event, the participants of which gain new knowledge and skills in the process of dynamic group work. Such events are useful for young professionals who want to learn new things in practice. The organizers are trying to give each participant a special attention, because the reputation of their company depends on it.

Round tables or expert evenings are the rarest, but also the most useful events. Experts from well-known companies are invited to such meetings so that they can answer questions or share interesting case studies. After completing a series of questions - answers, participants either disperse or begin to get to know each other, making new useful acquaintances.

Despite the fact that such events are increasingly being held, the organizers are funds and small and medium-sized businesses. They are not looking for regular members, but for new clients for themselves. It is understandable, until recently, to hold a full-fledged event was worth good money.

The situation began to change with the advent of various sites where ordinary users can tell about the upcoming event. They are happy not only to place in their section of the necessary information about the upcoming meeting, but also make a distribution to their users in your city.
