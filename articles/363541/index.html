Antibodies, or immunoglobulins, are synthesized by lymphocytes in response to the invasion of foreign substances or organisms. For each of the "attacking" viruses need special antibodies. Humanity has been using alien antibodies to fight infectious diseases for more than a hundred years. Since 1912, the blood plasma of patients with influenza has been transfused to sick patients. Years later, immune proteins saved people with Lassa fever, SARS and Ebola, and other diseases.

Researchers took human chromosomes 14 and 2, which contain genes for the creation of Hantavirus antibodies, and combined them into an artificial chromosome introduced into a cow. The genes of the cows responsible for the production of antibodies were suppressed, so that they began to produce immunoglobulins for humans.
