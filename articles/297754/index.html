ProPublica launches the first major site on the Dark Web



The so-called Dark Web, or the Invisible Network, which gained fame as a paradise for criminals and drug dealers, is slowly but surely beginning to take on the appearance of a web mirror with a greater emphasis on privacy protection. And now a non-profit news organization called ProPublica has joined the ranks of the Deep Web.

On Wednesday, ProPublica became the first popular digital publication to launch a version of its website that will operate using Tor, which allows you to browse websites anonymously, blog, chat online, etc. ProPublica explains its decision to go to the Internet underground ¬ªPrecisely in order to provide news readers with complete anonymity and protection of their private information.
