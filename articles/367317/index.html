Adobe changes the distribution terms for Adobe Flash Player



A post appeared on the Adobe Flash Player distribution page indicating that starting January 22, 2016, all links to distribution packages will be removed. Now the distribution of Adobe Flash Player in a corporate environment will require a special license. Instructions for obtaining such a license are available at www.adobe.com/products/players/flash-player-distribution.html .
Also, from December 1, 2015, you will need an AdobeID account to request a license.


UPD As rightly noted in the comments, the conditions have changed only for corporate users, for a personal installation of Adobe Flash Player will be available as before. For corporate users, this means that there will be no easy way to get the msi-package or sccm-package.
We'll have to invent something with an exe-package to distribute it through a GPO, or to refuse to distribute Flash Player in a corporate environment. I will probably follow the second path and gradually will convince colleagues of employees not to use flash.
