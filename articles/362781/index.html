The first smartphone Microsoft Lumia



Microsoft has released the first smartphone under its own brand. Until now, all Lumia models carried the logo of the good old Nokia, and now the first device came out, the name of which is missing the name of the Finnish company.

With a price of only 110 euros, the Microsoft Lumia 535 smartphone is designed for the lower price segment, that is, for budget shoppers. This is very relevant to the current crisis times.

The number "535" in the name in a strange way means three fives "5x5x5". Each of the "fives" has its own meaning.
  1. Five programs: Skype, Office, Cortana, OneDrive and Outlook (as on all Windows Phone devices)
  2. 5 megapixel front camera
  3. 5-inch screen (albeit with a weak resolution qHD, 960x540)

The smartphone used the Snapdragon 200 platform with a quad-core Cortex A7 CPUs (1.2 GHz), 1 GB of RAM and 8 GB of internal flash memory.

The 1905 mAh 3.7 V battery holds a little more than 7 Wh of energy.

The frontal selfie camera has a slight drawback: for some reason, it records video with a resolution of 848 x 480, no more.



It is planned to release two versions of the Lumia 535: one SIM-card and two. As usual, Lumia will be sold in different colors: bright green, orange, white, gray, black and blue. Sales will begin in "key markets" in November this year.
