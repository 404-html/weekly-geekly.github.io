"The universal language of science" through the mouth of Albert Einstein

Today I accidentally discovered a very interesting audio recording on the Web. This is a voice recording of Einstein reading the Universal Language of Science for the British Association for the Advancement of Science (in English). The recording was made in 1941. I think many would like to hear the voice of Einstein.

Unknown craftsmen also took a famous photo of the great scientist, and processed it so that it seems as if this is a video recording of how Einstein was giving a lecture. The work itself is relevant today, so listening can be not only interesting, but also useful.
