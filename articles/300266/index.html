Google played out in a "monopoly": The European Commission set to work on the spread of Android OS



EU regulators officially charged Google with violating antitrust laws. Today, the European Commission has published a message . European Commissioner for Competition Margaret Vestager said that Google had supplied the Android operating system to manufacturers of devices with special conditions, abusing its dominant position in the market.

The European Commission concluded that such actions by Google could be detrimental to consumers, as they stifle competition and limit innovation in the industry.

Together with the Google Play store, the company obliged partners to pre-install their other services. Moreover, partners had to place icons of services on especially "favorable" places of the screen. Google also demanded that vendors (or pay them extra for it) install their search engine and default browser.

The corporation made an agreement with partners in such a way that they would not have the right to produce smartphones with Android OS versions from any developers other than Google.
