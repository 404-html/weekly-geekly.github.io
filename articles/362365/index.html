Jupiter for a while turned into a giant Cyclops

Usually, the Hubble Space Telescope indulges astronomers with beautiful views of the outer planets of the solar system. This time, this view has turned out beautiful to the horror. The main thing is that he does not blink ...


Image rights: NASA, ESA, and A. Simon (Goddard Space Flight Center).

It happened on April 21, 2014. Hubble routinely took pictures of the Big Red Spot on Jupiter, when suddenly the shadow of the satellite Ganymede transforms the picture. Instantly, the shadow turned the BKP into something like a big eye with a radius of 16,000 kilometers. Good developments for Kubrikov films (with appropriate audio accompaniment).

The author of this masterpiece is the Hubble wide-angle camera 3.
